# Project Zomboid Tin Can Texture Mapping Notes

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Purpose:** Record the observed UV mapping for the vanilla tin can texture based on in-game testing.

---

# Texture Layout

The texture is divided into two major sections:

* **Left side:** Vertical rectangular strip.
* **Right side:** Two octagonal shapes with surrounding background.

Based on color-coded testing in-game, the mapping appears to be:

| Texture Region                                              | Maps To                         |
| ----------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Left rectangular strip                                      | Exterior side wall of the can   |
| Upper octagon                                               | Top lid (inside and outside)    |
| Lower octagon                                               | Bottom lid (inside and outside) |
| Right-side background (everything surrounding the octagons) | Interior side wall of the can   |

---

# Evidence

A test texture was created using highly distinctive colors:

* Exterior strip → **Red**
* Upper octagon → **Green**
* Lower octagon → **Blue**
* Remaining right-side area → **Yellow**

Observed results:

* The **green** texture appeared on the **top lid**, visible from both inside and outside.
* The **blue** texture appeared on the **bottom lid**, visible from both inside and outside.
* The **red** texture appeared on the **outside cylindrical wall** of the can.
* The **yellow** texture appeared on the **inside cylindrical wall** of the can.

---

# Conclusions

The lid textures are **shared** between the interior and exterior surfaces. There does **not** appear to be separate UV space for:

* Top inside vs. top outside
* Bottom inside vs. bottom outside

Instead, each lid uses a single shared texture.

The interior cylindrical wall has its own independent texture region, allowing it to be colored differently from the exterior wall.

---

# Implications for the Butter Candle Mod

This mapping is ideal for a butter candle or lamp item.

Recommended texture design:

* **Exterior strip:** Normal tin can appearance.
* **Upper octagon:** Metal rim/top of can.
* **Lower octagon:** Normal metal bottom.
* **Right-side background:** Bright yellow/orange (or another warm color) to simulate candlelight reflecting from the inside of the can.

Since the inside wall uses its own UV region, it can be made significantly brighter than the exterior without affecting the outside appearance.

---

# Remaining Unknowns

These observations have not yet been verified:

* Whether the interior wall uses the **entire** right-side background or only part of it.
* Whether any portions of the right-side background are unused UV space.
* Whether mipmapping or shading slightly alters the perceived brightness of the interior.

These could be determined with a second test texture that divides the right-side background into multiple distinct colors (for example, quadrants or horizontal bands). That would allow the interior wall's UV layout to be mapped much more precisely.
