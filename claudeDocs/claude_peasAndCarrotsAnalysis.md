# Growing Peas and Carrots in Project Zomboid

*Updated: August 21, 2025*

## Overview

This analysis provides detailed growing strategies for two of Project Zomboid's most reliable and valuable crops: Green Peas (Greenpeas) and Carrots. Both crops are excellent choices for beginning and experienced farmers, offering distinct advantages and complementary growing characteristics.

## Crop Configuration Comparison

### Green Peas (Greenpeas)

**Basic Properties:**
- **Growth Time**: 292 hours (12.2 days)
- **Water Level**: 70 (moderate)
- **Water Needed**: 80 (high requirement)
- **Yield**: 2-4 peas (base), up to 6-8 with good conditions
- **Harvest Position**: Mid-level (easier harvesting)

**Seasonal Growing:**
- **Sow Months**: February, March, April (early spring)
- **Best Month**: March (optimal conditions, health bonus)
- **Risk Month**: April (50% chance of cursed status)
- **Bad Months**: October, November, December, January (winter kill)

**Special Properties:**
- **Slugs Proof**: Naturally resistant to slug infestations
- **Moth Food**: Attracts moth pests (shared with brassicas)

### Carrots

**Basic Properties:**
- **Growth Time**: 432 hours (18 days) 
- **Water Level**: 30 (low requirement)
- **Water Needed**: 70 (moderate requirement)
- **Yield**: 3-6 carrots (base), up to 10-15 with optimal conditions
- **Harvest Position**: Ground level (standard)

**Seasonal Growing:**
- **Sow Months**: February, March, April, May, June, July (extended season)
- **Best Months**: February, June (optimal conditions)
- **Risk Month**: July (50% chance of cursed status)
- **Bad Months**: January, October, November, December
- **Hardy Feature**: `badMonthHardyLevel = 4` (survives bad months if stage 4+)

## Detailed Growing Analysis

### Growth Speed Comparison

**Green Peas: Fast-Growing Crop (292 hours)**
- Reaches maturity in approximately 12 days
- Quicker return on investment
- More growing cycles per season possible
- Earlier food security in survival situations

**Carrots: Slow-Growing Crop (432 hours)**
- Takes 18 days to reach maturity
- 48% longer growing period than peas
- Requires more planning and patience
- Higher yield compensates for longer growth time

### Water Management Strategies

**Green Peas - High Water Requirements:**
```
Initial Water: 70
Required Water: 80
Water Deficit Tolerance: ~8 points before stress
```

**Optimal Watering Strategy:**
- Maintain water levels between 80-100
- More frequent watering needed
- Monitor during dry periods closely
- Benefits significantly from rainfall

**Carrots - Efficient Water Usage:**
```
Initial Water: 30  
Required Water: 70
Water Deficit Tolerance: ~7 points before stress
```

**Optimal Watering Strategy:**
- Very drought-tolerant initially
- Lower water requirements throughout growth
- Less maintenance needed
- Efficient use of limited water resources

### Seasonal Optimization

**Green Peas - Early Spring Specialist:**

**Optimal Planting Schedule:**
- **Best Strategy**: Plant in early March for optimal growth
- **Growing Window**: February-April only (3-month window)
- **Quick Harvest**: Can complete 2-3 cycles if planted early
- **Winter Prep**: Must finish before October

**Advanced Timing:**
- Early February planting: Finishes late February/early March
- March planting: Best conditions, finishes mid-March
- April planting: Risky but possible, watch for cursed status

**Carrots - Extended Season Flexibility:**

**Optimal Planting Schedule:**
- **Best Strategy**: Plant in February or June for optimal conditions
- **Growing Window**: February-July (6-month window)
- **Cold Hardy**: Can survive bad months if grown to stage 4+
- **Winter Survival**: Mature carrots can survive winter if established

**Advanced Timing:**
- February planting: Excellent conditions, harvest in March
- June planting: Best summer conditions, harvest in July
- July planting: Risky but hardy plants may survive

### Disease and Pest Management

**Green Peas Disease Profile:**

**Resistances:**
- **Slugs Proof**: Complete immunity to slug infestations
- Natural resistance reduces pest pressure significantly

**Vulnerabilities:**
- **Moth Food**: Attracts moth pests (caterpillar damage)
- **Standard Diseases**: Susceptible to mildew, aphids, flies
- **High Water Needs**: More vulnerable to water stress diseases

**Management Strategy:**
- Companion plant away from other moth food crops (brassicas)
- Monitor for moth infestations during growing season
- Maintain optimal water levels to prevent stress-related diseases

**Carrots Disease Profile:**

**Resistances:**
- No specific disease immunities
- Lower water requirements reduce some stress-related diseases

**Vulnerabilities:**
- **Standard Disease Suite**: Susceptible to all four disease types
- **Long Growing Period**: More exposure time to disease spread
- **Root Crop Issues**: Ground-level harvest may increase contamination risk

**Management Strategy:**
- Use companion planting with pest-resistant herbs
- Monitor neighboring plants for disease spread
- Consider preventive treatments during long growing period

## Comparative Growing Strategies

### Resource Efficiency Analysis

**Green Peas - Quick Returns:**
- **Time to Food**: 12 days from seed to harvest
- **Water Efficiency**: High water usage but quick payoff
- **Skill Development**: Good for rapid farming skill gains
- **Risk Profile**: Lower risk due to short growing period

**Use Cases:**
- Emergency food production
- Early game survival
- Skill training crops
- Multiple harvest seasons

**Carrots - Long-term Investment:**
- **Time to Food**: 18 days from seed to harvest  
- **Water Efficiency**: Excellent water-to-food ratio
- **Skill Development**: Substantial XP from healthy long-term plants
- **Risk Profile**: Higher risk but hardy nature compensates

**Use Cases:**
- Stable food supply
- Water-scarce environments  
- Long-term base establishment
- High-yield farming operations

### Complementary Growing Systems

**Seasonal Rotation Strategy:**

**Early Spring (Feb-Apr):**
- **Primary**: Plant peas in March for optimal conditions
- **Secondary**: Plant carrots in February for summer harvest
- **Result**: Peas provide quick food while carrots develop

**Late Spring/Summer (May-Jul):**
- **Primary**: Plant carrots in June for optimal conditions
- **Secondary**: Harvest peas and replant if possible
- **Result**: Continuous food production throughout growing season

**Resource Allocation Strategy:**

**High Water Availability:**
- Focus on peas for quick returns
- Multiple pea harvests per season
- Use excess water efficiently

**Limited Water Resources:**
- Prioritize carrots for efficiency
- Supplement with drought-resistant herbs
- Maximize yield per water unit

### Companion Planting Recommendations

**Beneficial Companions for Both Crops:**

**Pest Control Plants:**
- **Chives**: Aphid control for both crops
- **Basil**: Fly control, complements growing seasons
- **Marigold**: General pest deterrent

**Strategic Placement:**
- Plant pest-control herbs adjacent to both crops
- Avoid planting peas near other moth food crops
- Create mixed plots for disease resistance

**Avoid These Combinations:**
- Peas + Brassicas (shared moth attraction)  
- Dense monocultures (disease spread risk)
- Plants with conflicting water needs in same area

## Advanced Farming Techniques

### Succession Planting

**Green Peas Succession:**
1. **First Planting**: Early February (finish before risk month)
2. **Second Planting**: Mid-March (optimal conditions)  
3. **Third Planting**: Early April (if weather permits)

**Benefits:**
- Continuous pea supply through spring
- Risk distribution across multiple plantings
- Maximizes short growing window

**Carrots Succession:**
1. **First Planting**: February (best month bonus)
2. **Second Planting**: June (second best month)
3. **Staggered Plantings**: Every 2-3 weeks for continuous harvest

**Benefits:**
- Steady carrot supply throughout season
- Spreads risk over long growing period
- Maximizes extended growing window

### Hardy Growing Techniques

**Carrot Winter Survival Strategy:**
- Plant carrots in early summer (May-June)
- Ensure plants reach stage 4+ before winter
- `badMonthHardyLevel = 4` allows winter survival
- Harvest in spring if plants survive

**Benefits:**
- Year-round food security
- Reduced spring planting pressure
- Maximum utilization of hardy trait

### Seed Production Strategies

**Green Peas Seed Production:**
- Allow plants to reach stage 6 (fully grown)
- Shorter seed production cycle due to fast growth
- Self-seeding capability with greenpeas as seeds
- Quick seed multiplication possible

**Carrots Seed Production:**  
- Longer commitment due to extended growth time
- Higher seed yield from larger plants
- Plan for 18+ days before seed harvest
- More substantial seed reserves when successful
